Osteopathy

Osteopathy is a practice that centers around manually adjusting the structure of the body to allow for proper self healing and regulating mechanisms to take place.

Osteopathy was founded in 1874 by Andrew Taylor Still in Kirksville Missouri. He founded this science based on principles that came out of his observation of natural law through the relationship between anatomy and physiology. Since its inception, there have been other health paradigms that have branched off from what is now referred to as "classical osteopathy" like visceral and cranial osteopathy, chiropractic, and sports medicine. Classical osteopathy is the renewal of the science that A.T. Still founded.

"An osteopath reasons from his knowledge of anatomy. He compares the work of the abnormal body with the normal body."
- Andrew Taylor Still

The Body As a Unit

The field of Osteopathy understands that body systems are all connected and nothing occurs inside the body in isolation. Whereas other health professionals will differentiate and specialize into cardiac, hormone, musculoskeletal, or gastrointestinal specialists, osteopathy seeks to build and treat connections between these bodily systems.

The Body's Healing and Regulatory Mechanisms

When you cut your finger, or catch a cold, your body utilizes intrinsic mechanisms that heal you. Osteopathy is about adjusting the body to allow for these natural processes to occur more freely.

The body's self healing mechanisms can be impaired by mechanical dysfunction. In the same way a kinked garden hose is unable to produce a flow of water, obstructions in the body prevent adequate movement of fluid and signal transduction of nerves in the body. The consequence of this impaired structure is that the body loses it's ability to maintain homeostasis and health.
