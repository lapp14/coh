Indian Head Massage (Champissage)

Traditionally known as Champissage in India, this massage included the head and hair and was practiced by women to promote blood flow to the scalp and healthy hair. Formalization of this technique, by Narendra Metha, was extended to include the neck, shoulders, upper arms and face, not only promoting hair growth, but providing relieve from stress induced aches, pains, eye strain, migraines and lower back pain.

This treatment is preformed overtop of clothes (preferably a T- Shirt) and without the use of oils, while sitting upright, therefore can be done on anyone at anytime. This therapy is highly recommended for individuals who spend hours in front of a computer.
